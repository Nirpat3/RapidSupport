Meta updated WhatsApp Business Solution terms effective Jan 15, 2026 to restrict “general-purpose AI assistants” from being distributed on WhatsApp. The good news: AI used to support your business’s customer service and sales workflows is still the normal/expected use case (agent + bot) — just don’t position it as an open-ended public AI assistant for anything/everything.  ￼

Also: WhatsApp has a strict 24-hour customer service window — inside it you can reply freely; outside it you must use approved templates.  ￼

WhatsApp rules you must design around

1) The 24-hour window
	•	When the user messages you, a 24-hour customer service window starts/reset.
	•	Inside the window: normal free-form chat is allowed.
	•	Outside the window: you can only re-engage using approved message templates.  ￼

So your portal should show a countdown timer and have:
	•	“Send template” button (re-open convo)
	•	Template library (support follow-up, demo reminder, onboarding checklist, etc.)

2) “Groups”

Most businesses should assume API-driven group messaging is not generally available like normal WhatsApp groups.
	•	Meta does have “group messaging” docs/paths, but eligibility can be restricted.  ￼
	•	Twilio explicitly notes WhatsApp “Groups API” was deprecated and they offer a workaround using their Conversations product (not a real WhatsApp group in the normal sense).  ￼

Best practical approach:
	•	Keep customers in 1:1 WhatsApp threads (clean, compliant, works great for support and sales)
	•	Inside your portal, allow multi-agent collaboration (so it feels like a group to your team)

If you want “community” style updates, do that via templates/broadcast-style campaigns (with opt-in) rather than true groups.
